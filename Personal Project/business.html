Creating your Network

Today, successful business development is contingent upon building trusted relationships and leveraging those relationships to meet new prospects. These relationships form the backbone of your business network. It is vital that you continuously grow and nurture these relationships, as a large percentage of your business growth will come from your business network.

Try Seeking Referrals
♦ Your customers certainly know colleagues like themselves who need your product or service. It is acceptable to ask them for a referral and request permission to use their name for an introduction. With a referred contact in hand and a trusted customer as back up, you can then make your telephone call or send an email to gain an introduction and you will have a much greater chance of success in your introductory contact.
Try Being Social and Sociable
♦ Be Social Grow your business network by using social media sites and apps, such as LinkedIn, Twitter, and online networking communities. However, before you jump into social media, you need to fully understand the medium, how it works, and how you can use it to benefit your business. LinkedIn is a great place to start and grow your business network. Twitter is a microblogging site that will complement your blog.
Create an interesting and direct Bio on social sites
♦ Creating a concise and interesting bio will determine the number of prospects you will attract. It als determine the type of people that will show interest. You should regularly update this information as you business start to grow and evolve
Network!!!
♦ Social events are one of the most natural ways to meet and network with new people, while helping you build tangible, human connections. Try your best to recognise the opportunities when they present themselves. Treat networking, and social networking, like you would a meaningful friendship. When you forge these relationships, the concept of networking doesn’t exist. Instead of false handshakes, awkward communication, and empty promises, you’ll earn key business contacts that you actually care about, and who care about you and your business.

Reaching Your Audience

  1. Know your Target Audience - To reach your target audience, you must first define who is your target audience. You can do this by building a consumer persona. A customer or buyer persona is a generalized representation of your ideal buyers. Then you can market your brand and products only to those who are most likely to be genuinely interested in them. This means that there will be higher chances of getting leads and conversions. This will help you increase revenue by investing less.

  2. Focus on customer's interest - The best way to reach your target audience is by providing them with relevant content. Writing about topics that are of interest to your intended audience is a sure way of grabbing their attention. The more targeted and relevant your content is, the easier it will be for you to reach your target audience and engage them. Content marketing plays a major role in helping you generate leads and conversions.

  3. Survey your customers - There are many ways to reach your target market more effectively and the first is to survey customers. Surveying current customers you wish to target will give you a better idea about your product or service, what’s missing from it and how you can improve it to make your customers more satisfied. Make sure you include not only your current customers but anyone you think may be interested in what you offer and use the data you collect to shape your brand to better fit with your target market.

  4. Using LinkedIn - Updating your linkedin profile is a great way to personally advertise your business.Use your LinkedIn network to introduce yourself to influencers in your industry. Share content and interact with posts from other members in your industry. LinkedIn is also a powerful tool to connect with future prospects. You can use LinkedIn's built-in search tool to find your target audience and then send connection requests. Don't just sit and wait for people to add you. Take charge and go after the audience you want in your network — just make sure your LinkedIn profile is complete with your latest information.
